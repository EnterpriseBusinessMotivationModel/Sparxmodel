This viewpoint is useful to focus on the relationship between strategies, processes, and metrics.  Any strategy that is not described in terms of a success metric is not actionable.  Therefore, it is the job of the business architect to insure that strategies either break down to objectives that can be measured or directly contain the metrics.  <br/><br/>Not all success metrics can be directly tracked by the business.  Some need an internal metric that serves as a proxy for the actual success metric.   That is a Key Performance Indicator.  In those cases where a business objective can be directly described by an internal success metric, then the KPI is the same as the success metric (the metric tracks itself).<br/><br/>Note that a KPI is a process metric, but not all process metrics are KPIs.  You can measure many things about a process.  There can be many reasons for measurement.  However, some metrics are purely supporting, and would not be a metric useful for tracking success.  <p/>
