When a capability is defined in an organization, there are expectations of the level of performance or maturity required in order for the business to be successful in their mission.  An assessment metric defines a way in which a set of capabilities will be measured.  For example, if all capabilities will be measured on the basis of their value, performance, and maturity (VPM), then there are three assessment metrics.<br/><br/>Capturing the measures for an assessment metric is a key element of business architecture.  The measurement process must be consistent and, in the best case, should reference widely accepted business measurement practices.  <br/><br/>In order for cross-enterprise assessment to take place in a timely and effective manner, it is a best practice for a single team to define a standard set of capability assessment metrics that will be used by every individual that performs the analysis.<br/><br/>Assessment measures are used to help the business to <br/><ol>
<li>visualize the gaps between expected and actual performance, </li><li>place appropriate prioritization on the capabilities that need attention, and </li><li>evaluate the current allocation of resources in budgets and program alignments to the areas with the greatest gaps.</li></ol>
<br/>In other words, if ten capabilities are important to enabling a strategy, the data related to their required performance and maturity can assist the organization in deciding which one of those capabilities should receive investment in order to be effective fulfilling the strategy.<br/><br/>Some examples of assessment metrics may include: the business-perceived value of a capability, the level of expected performance against a particular set of key performance indicators (KPIs), the incidence rate of defects or variations, the incremental and overall cost of providing the capability, the number of resources involved, the level of automation and/or integration, the risk of business interruption (for disaster recovery) and the likelihood of customer impact if performance metrics change.<p/>
