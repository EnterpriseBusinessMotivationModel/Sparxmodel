Strategy is a complex set of related statements used to motivate the creation of projects, the setting of goals, and the achievement of objectives by employees and partners of an enterprise in support of a business goal.  It is not a course of action in itself, but a instead provides the general outlines of a course of action sufficient to drive specific changes in business operations.  Strategies are defined in terms of objectives.  <br/><br/><br/>A strategy motivates change. It is the means by which an goal is to be reached.  Rarely a statement of continuity, a strategy describes one or more specific changes that should be made to the enterprise. Those changes may be made to any element of the enterprise, including to the business model, the business capabilities, the structure of the business units, and even the technologies used to manage information.<br/><br/>A strategy may enable another strategy (If the widget department meets their objectives, then we can drop our reworking costs by 15% in the third month.)<br/><br/>In addition, a strategy may influence another strategy: ("With the push for quality in the widget division, the thingamabob division has set new goals for 2012.").  <br/><br/>Strategies may be inspired directly by influencers like competitive pressure or business trends or they may be suggested in a business model assessment.  It is important to know the source of the strategy and what it is responding to, in order to understand relative importance and check the assumptions on which a strategy is based.<br/><br/>A strategy may operate at any level of the organization from the most broadly encompassing enterprise wide level to the department or team level.  <p/>
