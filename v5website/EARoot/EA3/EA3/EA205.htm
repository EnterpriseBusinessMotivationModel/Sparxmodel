<p>A Legislative Edict is a requirement, placed by a legislative body, forcing <u>change </u>on an organization.  Typically used to regulate government agencies, legislative edicts can be placed on commercial organizations as well (example: Sarbanes-Oxley Financial Regulations).  A legislative edict is different from a regulation, in that a regulation forces ongoing measurement, while an edict forces change.<br/></p>
