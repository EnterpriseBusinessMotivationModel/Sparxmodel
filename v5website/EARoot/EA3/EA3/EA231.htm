<p>An analysis based on Porter's Five Forces looks at individual businesses or "strategic business units", examining the threats that lead to a rich understanding of the competitive and cooperative environment in which a business operates.<br/></p><p><br/></p><p>The power of suppliers tends to be a reversal of the power of buyers.<br/></p><p><ul><li>Where the switching costs are high e.g. Switching from one software supplier to another. </li><br/></p><p><li>Power is high where the brand is powerful e.g. Cadillac, Pizza Hut, Microsoft. </li><br/></p><p><li>There is a possibility of the supplier integrating forward e.g. Brewers buying bars. </li><br/></p><p><li>Customers are fragmented (not in clusters) so that they have little bargaining power e.g. Gas/Petrol stations in remote places. </li><br/></p><p></ul><br/></p><p>The factors that affect supplier power are:<br/></p><p><ul><li>Supplier concentration</li><br/></p><p><li>Importance of volume to supplier</li><br/></p><p><li>Differentiation of inputs</li><br/></p><p><li>Impact of inputs on cost or differentiation</li><br/></p><p><li>Switching costs of firms in the industry</li><br/></p><p><li>Presence of substitute inputs</li><br/></p><p><li>Threat of forward integration</li><br/></p><p><li>Cost relative to total purchases in industry</li><br/></p><p></ul><br/></p>
