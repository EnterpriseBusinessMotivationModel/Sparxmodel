<p>A business model assessment is an artifact or document that contains an analysis of one (or more) of the business models of an enterprise.  It may be formal, with hundreds or thousands of typed pages, or informal, delivered to an executive over lunch at the golf course.  <br/></p><p><br/></p><p>Business model assessments are composed of a set of <i>judgments </i>about one or more influencers that affect the organizations ability to execute on  the existing business model.   Business judgements are produced as the result of an assessment process, which is an examination of one or more of the business models in use in the enterprise in light of a dynamic business environment.  <br/></p><p><br/></p><p>Business judgements in the business model assessment may motivate the creation of principles, goals and/or strategies that either directly modify the business model or influence how the model is being executed upon.  <br/></p>
