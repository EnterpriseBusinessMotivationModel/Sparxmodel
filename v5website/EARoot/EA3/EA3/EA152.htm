<p>a brand perception is said to "overlay" an offering in that the customers perception of the brand will impact how they perceive an offering and the value that they expect to get from it.  The two elements are distinct, however, in that an offering is very transitive and represents something that a customer can buy, while a brand perception may be built up over time.<br/></p>

<p>A brand perception could be said to overlay an instance of a business model, as offered by a particular enterprise, in that the perception influences how the customer expects to collect value from the products and services offered by a particular business model of an enterprise.<br/></p>
