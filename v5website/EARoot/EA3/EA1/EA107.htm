<p>The key to understanding the Strategy view is to realize that a strategy is not everything.  It sits in an environment of information and ideas, often in the heads of stakeholders.  A strategy is simply one element, although an essential one.  <br/></p><p><br/></p><p>The business model describes how the business is supposed to be, but often it does not describe how it currently is. This can happen because the model was changed by executives, but the company is taking time to adapt and change to the new model.  Or, perhaps, the model is an idealistic view, while the actual organization is exactly optimum.  <br/></p><p><br/></p><p>A strategy, then, can define changes to the organization either for the purpose of making it more like the business model (to complete a transition) or less like the operating model (to optimize for reality).  Regardless of why a strategy exists, is always exists in the context of the business model.<br/></p>
